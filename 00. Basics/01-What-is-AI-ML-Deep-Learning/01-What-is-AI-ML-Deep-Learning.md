# What is AI · Machine Learning · Deep Learning
> The big picture before anything else: **Artificial Intelligence** is the goal (machines that act
> intelligently), **Machine Learning** is the dominant way we get there (learn patterns from data
> instead of hand-coding rules), and **Deep Learning** is the ML technique — many-layered neural
> networks — that powers today's breakthroughs. AI ⊃ ML ⊃ DL.

**Why it matters:** "What's the difference between AI, ML, and deep learning?" is the most common
opening question in any ML interview or first lecture. Getting the nested relationship right — and
knowing *why* we switched from rules to learning from data — is the foundation everything else sits on.

**⭐ Start here — suggested path:**

1. **Get the one-sentence mental model** — watch [Machine Learning Explained in 100 Seconds](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PeMlggyqz0Y). *Fastest possible "what is ML" before any detail.*
2. **See the nesting** — read [Google: What is Machine Learning?](https://developers.google.com/machine-learning/intro-to-ml/what-is-ml). *Pins down AI ⊃ ML ⊃ DL and rules-vs-learning, authoritatively.*
3. **Build real intuition** — watch [3Blue1Brown: But what is a neural network?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aircAruvnKk). *Shows what "deep learning" actually computes, visually.*
4. **Hear it from Andrew Ng** — [What is Machine Learning?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ukzFI9rgwfU). *The canonical framing from the person who taught most of the field.*
5. **Place it in the AI landscape** — skim [Elements of AI — What is AI?](https://course.elementsofai.com/1/1). *Free university chapter that situates ML inside the broader AI story.*

- [Computing Machinery and Intelligence](https://courses.cs.umbc.edu/471/papers/turing.pdf) — **Alan Turing (1950)** — the founding question "can machines think?" and the Turing Test.
- [A Few Useful Things to Know About Machine Learning](https://homes.cs.washington.edu/~pedrod/papers/cacm12.pdf) — **Pedro Domingos (2012)** — the single best plain-English overview of what ML is and isn't.
- [Deep Learning (Nature review)](https://www.nature.com/articles/nature14539) — **LeCun, Bengio & Hinton (2015)** — the authoritative "what deep learning is and why it works" overview.

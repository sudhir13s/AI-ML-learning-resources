# Jupyter & Google Colab
> Notebooks are where ML actually gets done. **Jupyter** lets you mix code, output, plots, and notes
> in one document, running cell by cell so you can explore data interactively. **Google Colab** is a
> free, zero-setup Jupyter in the browser — with **free GPUs/TPUs** — so you can train models without
> installing anything. This is the everyday workbench for data science and deep learning.

**Why it matters:** nearly every ML tutorial, Kaggle kernel, and research demo ships as a notebook,
and Colab's free GPU is how most beginners first train a real neural network. Knowing the cell-based
workflow (and its pitfalls, like out-of-order execution) makes you productive immediately.

**⭐ Start here — suggested path:**

1. **Learn the notebook workflow** — watch [Jupyter Notebook Tutorial (Corey Schafer)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HW29067qVWk). *Cells, kernels, run order — the core mental model.*
2. **Try it with zero setup** — open [Colab: Welcome notebook](https://colab.research.google.com/notebooks/intro.ipynb) and run a cell. *The fastest possible "I'm doing ML" moment.*
3. **Get comfortable in Colab** — watch [Get started with Google Colab](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=inN8seMm7UI). *Uploading data, installing packages, and enabling the free GPU.*
4. **Read the official basics** — skim [Jupyter docs](https://docs.jupyter.org/en/latest/) and the [Colab FAQ](https://research.google.com/colaboratory/faq.html). *Authoritative answers on runtimes, files, and limits.*
5. **Adopt good habits** — restart-and-run-all before sharing; keep cells in order. *Avoids the classic "works on my machine because of hidden state" bug.*

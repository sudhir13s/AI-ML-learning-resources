# Pandas Essentials
> Pandas is how you load, clean, slice, and reshape tabular data — the format most ML datasets arrive
> in. The **DataFrame** (a table) and **Series** (a column) are your everyday tools for reading CSVs,
> handling missing values, filtering, grouping, and joining. Most of a real ML project is Pandas work
> *before* a model ever sees the data.

**Why it matters:** since data prep dominates ML, Pandas fluency is what makes you productive.
Interviews and take-homes constantly ask you to "load this CSV, clean it, and engineer a few features" —
which is `read_csv`, `groupby`, `merge`, boolean indexing, and `apply` in practice.

**⭐ Start here — suggested path:**

1. **Get the DataFrame model** — read [Pandas: 10 minutes to pandas](https://pandas.pydata.org/docs/user_guide/10min.html). *Series, DataFrame, indexing — the mental model.*
2. **Practice on real data** — do [Kaggle Learn: Pandas](https://www.kaggle.com/learn/pandas). *Hands-on filtering, grouping, and combining — the skills you'll use daily.*
3. **Watch a complete tour** — [Complete Pandas Data Science Tutorial (Keith Galli)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vmEHCJofslg). *Reading files, filtering, groupby, and more, with a real dataset.*
4. **Go deeper on the patterns** — [Corey Schafer: Pandas (Part 1)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZyhVh-qRZPA). *A careful, well-structured series that builds real fluency.*
5. **Keep the cookbook open** — bookmark the [Getting started tutorials](https://pandas.pydata.org/docs/getting_started/intro_tutorials/index.html). *Task-oriented recipes for the operations you'll forget.*

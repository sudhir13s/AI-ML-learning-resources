# Building a "Stay-Current" Routine
> A sustainable weekly system that ties together everything in this section — read papers, find/track
> research, follow people, watch benchmarks — into a habit you can actually keep. The goal is signal
> per hour: a small, repeatable loop that keeps you current without drowning in the firehose or burning out.

**Why it matters:** staying current isn't an event, it's a habit, and the people who keep up over
years are the ones with a *system*, not the ones who binge then quit. A defined routine (skim weekly,
deep-read occasionally, anchor to fundamentals) is what makes "how do you stay current?" a confident
interview answer instead of "I try to read Twitter."

**⭐ Start here — suggested path:**

1. **Set a budget and a cadence** — decide your weekly hours up front (e.g. 1 skim hour + 1 deep hour). *A fixed budget turns "keep up with everything" into a solvable, repeatable task.*
2. **Build the loop: skim → triage → deep-read** — weekly skim [HF Daily Papers](https://huggingface.co/papers) + one newsletter, triage to a shortlist, deep-read 1 paper. *This chains cards 01–06 into one habit: discover, filter, understand.*
3. **Watch the pros' routines** — see ⭐ [How to stay up-to-date without losing your mind](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KIm70L1X32E) and steal a workflow. *Borrowing a working routine beats inventing one; tweak from there.*
4. **Anchor new ideas to fundamentals** — when something new lands, map it onto stable concepts (transformers, optimization, eval) instead of treating it as novel. *Anchoring prevents hype whiplash and makes new methods easy to file mentally.*
5. **Review and prune monthly** — drop sources you skim without learning; protect deep-read time. *A routine only survives if it stays small and high-signal; pruning is maintenance, not failure.*

## 📄 Key Papers
- [How to Read a Paper](http://ccr.sigcomm.org/online/files/p83-keshavA.pdf) — **S. Keshav (2007)** — the three-pass method that makes the "deep-read" step time-bounded.
- [Troubling Trends in Machine Learning Scholarship](https://arxiv.org/abs/1807.03341) — **Lipton & Steinhardt (2018)** — what to stay skeptical of so your routine builds judgment, not just awareness.
- [Deep Learning (review)](https://www.nature.com/articles/nature14539) — **LeCun, Bengio & Hinton (2015)** — the kind of stable anchor to map new work onto.

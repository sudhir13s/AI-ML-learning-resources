# Newsletters & Blogs to Follow
> The curated, high-signal channels that pre-digest the firehose for you — weekly research roundups,
> deep technical explainers, and official lab blogs. The goal is a small subscription list (1–2
> newsletters + 2–3 explainer blogs) you can skim weekly, not a feed you drown in.

**Why it matters:** you can't read every paper, but you can follow people whose job is to read them
for you. Knowing the difference between a *roundup* (breadth, low effort) and a *deep explainer*
(depth, high signal) — and picking one of each — is how working engineers stay current sustainably.

**⭐ Start here — suggested path:**

1. **Subscribe to one weekly roundup** — pick ⭐ [The Batch](https://www.deeplearning.ai/the-batch/) (accessible) or [Import AI](https://importai.net/) (research + policy, low hype). *One roundup, skimmed weekly, keeps you aware of what happened without daily noise.*
2. **Add one deep explainer** — follow [Lil'Log (Lilian Weng)](https://lilianweng.github.io/) and [Ahead of AI (Sebastian Raschka)](https://magazine.sebastianraschka.com/). *When something matters, these go deep with correct math and code — your "read once, understand fully" source.*
3. **Bookmark official lab blogs** — [DeepMind](https://deepmind.google/discover/blog/), [Anthropic Research](https://www.anthropic.com/research), [Google Research](https://research.google/blog/), [BAIR](https://bair.berkeley.edu/blog/). *Go to the source for releases instead of secondhand summaries.*
4. **Keep a "visual intuition" blog on hand** — [Jay Alammar](https://jalammar.github.io/) and [colah's blog](https://colah.github.io/). *When a new method confuses you, these illustrated explainers rebuild the mental model.*
5. **Prune ruthlessly** — after a month, drop anything you skim without learning. *A 4-item list you actually read beats a 30-item feed you ignore. Curate for signal.*

## 🎓 Courses (free)
- [fast.ai](https://www.fast.ai/) — **Jeremy Howard & Rachel Thomas** — the blog/courses model "practitioner-first" current-ML; subscribe for posts that translate research into practice.
- [DeepLearning.AI — The Batch](https://www.deeplearning.ai/the-batch/) — **Andrew Ng** — a weekly newsletter that doubles as an ongoing, free short-course in what's changing.

## 🎥 Videos
- [How to Stay Up-to-date in AI/ML Without Losing Your Mind](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KIm70L1X32E) — **Marina Wyss** — how to choose a small set of newsletters/blogs and a sustainable cadence.
- [How to Stay Up to Date on ML Research](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b1iz0l5KYig) — **Super Data Science (Jon Krohn)** — concrete sources and a weekly reading routine.
- [How To Read AI Research Papers Effectively](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K6Wui3mn-uI) — **DeepLearning.AI** — pairs with following blogs: when a roundup flags a paper, read it well.
- [Read a research paper effectively — tools and tricks](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g8qatelVS7c) — **Andy Stapleton** — discovery + triage tooling that complements a subscription list.

## 📄 Key Papers
- [Attention? Attention! (Lil'Log)](https://lilianweng.github.io/posts/2018-06-24-attention/) — **Lilian Weng** — the canonical example of a blog post that explains a research thread better than most surveys.
- [The Annotated Transformer](https://nlp.seas.harvard.edu/annotated-transformer/) — **Harvard NLP** — a blog-as-paper: the Transformer paper reimplemented inline; the gold standard for "explainer blog."
- [Troubling Trends in Machine Learning Scholarship](https://arxiv.org/abs/1807.03341) — **Lipton & Steinhardt (2018)** — why secondhand summaries can mislead; read the source when it matters.

## 📰 Articles / Blogs (free, no paywall)
- [Lil'Log](https://lilianweng.github.io/) — **Lilian Weng** — deep, authoritative explainers of new methods (RLHF, diffusion, agents).
- [Ahead of AI](https://magazine.sebastianraschka.com/) — **Sebastian Raschka** — research + practice, well-sourced (free posts).
- [Import AI](https://importai.net/) — **Jack Clark** — weekly research + policy roundup, deliberately low-hype.
- [The Gradient](https://thegradient.pub/) — **The Gradient** — long-form, editorially-reviewed essays on ML research and its direction.
- [Interconnects](https://www.interconnects.ai/) — **Nathan Lambert** — open RLHF/post-training and frontier-model analysis (free posts).

## 📚 Books (free, with chapters)
- [Distill](https://distill.pub/) — **Distill** — interactive, peer-reviewed explanatory articles; the bar for what a great technical explainer looks like.
- [Dive into Deep Learning](https://d2l.ai/) — **Zhang et al.** — when a blog post sparks interest, the matching d2l chapter gives you runnable depth.

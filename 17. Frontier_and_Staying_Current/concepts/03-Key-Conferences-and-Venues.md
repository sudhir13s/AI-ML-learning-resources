# Key Conferences & Venues
> The peer-reviewed venues where ML research is vetted and presented — what each is known for, how
> to read their proceedings for free, and how to use acceptance/Test-of-Time signals to separate
> durable work from noise. Conferences (not journals) are the primary venues in ML, and almost all
> proceedings are open access.

**Why it matters:** "where would this be published / is this peer-reviewed?" is a credibility check
you'll make constantly. Knowing that NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR are the general "Big Three," that ACL/EMNLP
own NLP and CVPR/ICCV/ECCV own vision, and that proceedings + OpenReview reviews are free, lets you
go straight to vetted sources instead of secondhand summaries.

**⭐ Start here — suggested path:**

1. **Learn the landscape** — read [Beyond NeurIPS: a student's guide to AI conferences](https://algoverseairesearch.org/blog/icml-iclr-aaai-student-guide) and note who owns which subfield. *The "Big Three" are general ML; subfields have their own top venues (ACL/EMNLP, CVPR/ICCV/ECCV).*
2. **Read proceedings for free** — open ⭐ [NeurIPS Proceedings](https://proceedings.neurips.cc/), [PMLR (ICML/AISTATS)](https://proceedings.mlr.press/), and the [ACL Anthology](https://aclanthology.org/). *Every paper is a free PDF; this is the primary source, not the press coverage.*
3. **Read the reviews, not just the paper** — browse a paper on [OpenReview](https://openreview.net/) and read the reviewer–author discussion. *Seeing what reviewers pushed back on is a fast-track to critical reading and "hype vs substance."*
4. **Use durability signals** — look for **Test-of-Time** and **Best Paper** awards and high acceptance-venue provenance. *Awards and venue tier are crude but useful priors for what will still matter in 5 years.*
5. **Watch the talks** — most conferences post oral/spotlight talks and tutorials free on YouTube; queue a few per year. *Talks compress a paper into 15 minutes and surface the intuition.*

## 🎓 Courses (free)
- [Stanford CS25: Transformers United](https://web.stanford.edu/class/cs25/) — **Stanford** — a free seminar of frontier guest lectures (open Zoom + YouTube), a great proxy for "what's hot at the top venues."
- [ICML / NeurIPS / ICLR Tutorials](https://icml.cc/) — **ICML / NeurIPS / ICLR** — conference tutorial tracks (posted free) are expert-curated surveys of a hot area.

## 📄 Key Papers
- [NeurIPS Proceedings](https://proceedings.neurips.cc/) — **NeurIPS** — open-access PDFs for every accepted paper since 1987.
- [PMLR — Proceedings of Machine Learning Research](https://proceedings.mlr.press/) — **PMLR** — open proceedings for ICML, AISTATS, CoLT, and more.
- [ACL Anthology](https://aclanthology.org/) — **ACL** — the open archive of NLP papers (ACL, EMNLP, NAACL, etc.).
- [CVF Open Access](https://openaccess.thecvf.com/) — **CVF** — free proceedings for CVPR, ICCV, and WACV.

## 📰 Articles / Blogs (free, no paywall)
- [Beyond NeurIPS — a student's guide to AI conferences](https://algoverseairesearch.org/blog/icml-iclr-aaai-student-guide) — **Algoverse** — what each major venue is known for and how they differ.
- [OpenReview](https://openreview.net/) — **OpenReview** — read the actual reviews/rebuttals behind ICLR (and others) to see how work is vetted.
- [Journal-to-Conference Track (NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR)](https://neurips.cc/public/JournalToConference) — **NeurIPS** — how the top venues coordinate; useful context for where work appears.
- [TMLR — Transactions on Machine Learning Research](https://jmlr.org/tmlr/) — **JMLR** — a fast, open, rigorous journal venue gaining prominence alongside the conferences.

## 📚 Books (free, with chapters)
- [JMLR — Journal of Machine Learning Research](https://www.jmlr.org/) — **JMLR** — the premier open-access ML journal; its archive is a free, citable reference shelf.
- [Dive into Deep Learning](https://d2l.ai/) — **Zhang et al.** — when a conference paper is dense, d2l's chapters are the readable, runnable companion.

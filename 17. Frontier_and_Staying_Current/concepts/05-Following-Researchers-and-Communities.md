# Following Researchers & Communities
> Build a personal "early-warning network" of researchers and communities who surface and debate new
> work before the newsletters catch up. Following the right people is the lowest-effort, highest-signal
> way to discover what matters — papers come with context, critique, and threads instead of just titles.

**Why it matters:** the people who built a method are the best filter for what's actually important
in it. A curated follow list (a few researchers + 1–2 communities) gives you the discussion *around*
a paper — the caveats, the failed replications, the "this is overhyped" replies — which is exactly
the judgment interviewers probe with "is this result trustworthy?"

**⭐ Start here — suggested path:**

1. **Follow a few primary-source researchers** — start with people who post their own work and explain it (e.g. via their lab blogs: [Lilian Weng](https://lilianweng.github.io/), [Sebastian Raschka](https://sebastianraschka.com/blog/), [Sebastian Ruder](https://www.ruder.io/)). *Following authors gives you papers *with* the author's own framing and caveats.*
2. **Join one discussion community** — lurk in ⭐ [r/MachineLearning](https://www.reddit.com/r/MachineLearning/) and the [Hugging Face forums](https://discuss.huggingface.co/). *Threads pre-filter what's interesting and surface the criticism a press release won't.*
3. **Watch a curator who critiques** — subscribe to [Yannic Kilcher](https://www.youtube.com/@YannicKilcher) for fast, opinionated paper breakdowns. *A skeptical curator models "hype vs substance" in real time.*
4. **Follow open-research orgs** — track [EleutherAI](https://www.eleuther.ai/) and [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/) for open models, datasets, and reproductions. *Open orgs publish the artifacts (code/weights) plus the discussion, not just claims.*
5. **Curate, then prune** — keep the follow list small and drop anyone who's mostly hype. *Signal density matters more than count; an early-warning network only works if you actually read it.*

## 🎓 Courses (free)
- [Hugging Face Learn](https://huggingface.co/learn) — **Hugging Face** — community-built courses; the forums and Discord around them are an active researcher/practitioner community.
- [fast.ai](https://www.fast.ai/) — **Jeremy Howard & Rachel Thomas** — a course *and* a community (forums) where practitioners discuss current work.

## 🎥 Videos
- [Yannic Kilcher — paper breakdowns (channel)](https://www.youtube.com/@YannicKilcher) — **Yannic Kilcher** — fast, opinionated reviews; a model of a researcher-curator to follow.
- [How to Stay Up-to-date in AI/ML Without Losing Your Mind](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KIm70L1X32E) — **Marina Wyss** — how to pick people/communities and avoid feed overload.
- [How to Stay Up to Date on ML Research](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b1iz0l5KYig) — **Super Data Science (Jon Krohn)** — using social + community channels as a discovery layer.
- [How To Read AI Research Papers Effectively](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K6Wui3mn-uI) — **DeepLearning.AI** — once your network surfaces a paper, read it well.

## 📄 Key Papers
- [Lil'Log — research-grade explainers](https://lilianweng.github.io/) — **Lilian Weng** — an example of following a researcher whose posts are as rigorous as surveys.
- [On Word Embeddings (series)](https://www.ruder.io/word-embeddings-1/) — **Sebastian Ruder** — a researcher's blog that became a canonical reference; the payoff of following primary sources.
- [Troubling Trends in Machine Learning Scholarship](https://arxiv.org/abs/1807.03341) — **Lipton & Steinhardt (2018)** — why community discussion (not just headlines) is where careful reading happens.

## 📰 Articles / Blogs (free, no paywall)
- [r/MachineLearning](https://www.reddit.com/r/MachineLearning/) — **Reddit** — discussion, paper threads, and "[D]" debates that surface critique.
- [Hugging Face forums](https://discuss.huggingface.co/) — **Hugging Face** — practitioner Q&A and model/dataset discussion.
- [EleutherAI](https://www.eleuther.ai/) — **EleutherAI** — an open-research community; their Discord and blog are a window into live research.
- [The Gradient](https://thegradient.pub/) — **The Gradient** — community-written long-form essays and interviews with researchers.

## 📚 Books (free, with chapters)
- [Distill](https://distill.pub/) — **Distill** — a community standard for clarity; following its authors leads to the best explainers in the field.
- [Dive into Deep Learning](https://d2l.ai/) — **Zhang et al.** — an open, community-maintained book; its forums discuss the methods you'll see researchers post about.
